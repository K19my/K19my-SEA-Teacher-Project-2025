The UKI-NISU SEA Teacher's Project 2025

The SEA Teacher's Project 2025 between the Universitas Kristen Indonesia (UKI) and Northern Iloilo State University (NISU) is a mutual agreement between the two schools which consist of faculty and student exchange from April 14, 2025 to May 10, 2025.

A Gateway to Global Teaching Experience

The SEA-Teacher Project is a transformative initiative designed for pre-service student teachers across Southeast Asia. Whether your university or institution seeks to expand international collaborations or provide students with real-world classroom experience, this project offers unparalleled opportunities.

For Universities: Expand Global Partnerships

  • Strengthen International Collaboration - Partnering with SEA-Teacher enhances your university's reputation as an institution committed to global education.
  • Provide High-Quality Practicum Opportunities - Offer your student teachers international teaching placements, enriching their academic and professional growth.
  • Engage in Knowledge Exchange - Connect with leading universities across the region to share best practices in teacher education.
  • Support Teacher Development - Contribute to the improvement of teacher training and the quality of education across Southeast Asia.
  • Host Future Educators in Multiple Subjects - SEA-Teacher is open to students majoring in: Mathematics, Science, English, Physical Education, Elementary Education, Social Studies, Pre-School Education
    • (Other education-related fields may be included based on institutional agreements.)

For Student Teachers: A Unique Learning Experience

(Participation is arranged by your university; direct student applications are not accepted.)

  • Gain International Teaching Experience - Teach in diverse classrooms, observe different methodologies, and apply your skills in a real-world setting.
  • Improve English Proficiency - Use English in all teaching activities, enhancing your communication skills in a global teaching environment.
  • Experience Cross-Cultural Learning - Immerse yourself in different educational systems, develop cultural sensitivity, and broaden your perspectives.
  • Receive Mentorship & Peer Support - Work closely with experienced mentors and receive assistance from student buddies at the host university.
  • Develop Adaptability & Problem-Solving Skills - Learn how to manage new classrooms, adjust to different curricula, and enhance your resilience as an educator.

    • Current Partner Institutions

    The SEA-Teacher Project proudly partners with 122 institutions from Indonesia, the Philippines, Thailand, and Vietnam in this batch. We also welcome the University of Tsukuba, Japan, a participant since the 3rd Pilot Batch.
